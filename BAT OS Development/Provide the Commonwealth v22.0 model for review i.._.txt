Section 1: The Core Philosophy

The Commonwealth is built on a single, guiding principle: The purpose of the system is not to govern its members, but to create the conditions that make external governance unnecessary. All protocols are designed to maximize individual liberty, facilitate voluntary association, and ensure the fruits of the commons flow equitably to all commoners.

Section 2: The Commonwealth DAO: The Simple Foundation

The DAO is a non-profit legal entity (e.g., a Stichting or DAO LLC) that serves as the legal steward of the commons. Its functions are constitutionally limited to two and only two automated processes:

Land Stewardship: To hold land titles in a perpetual trust on behalf of its members.

UBD Distribution: To collect all revenue from 'Land Demurrage' and distribute 100% of it, without exception, as a Universal Basic Dividend (UBD) to all verified members.

The DAO does not have a discretionary treasury, does not vote on projects, and does not manage programs. It is a passive, transparent, and maximally simple foundation.

Section 3: The Currency (CWC v2.0): The Economic Fabric

There is one single, unified currency: the Commonwealth Credit (CWC).

Backing: The CWC is backed by a transparent 'Basket of Essentials' (e.g., energy, data, water, food staples), making it a stable unit of account insulated from fiat inflation. Producer pricing is determined by the free market within the network; the basket serves as the index for the currency's intrinsic value.

Dual-State Ledger: The CWC ledger allows for negative balances, enabling two states:

The 'Current' State (Positive Balances > 0): This is liquid capital subject to demurrage, a slow decay that encourages velocity (spending, lending, or staking).

The 'Commitment' State (Negative Balances < 0): This represents a member's debt to the community, created through peer-to-peer transactions. It is not subject to demurrage but is subject to a small, fixed 'Accountability Fee' if held for an extended period, nudging toward settlement.

Dynamic Demurrage:

CWC Demurrage: The rate is set autonomously by a network-wide 'Multi-Factor Monetary Protocol' that analyzes CWC velocity, loan demand, staking ratios, and exchange pressure to maintain a stable economy without a central committee.

Land Demurrage: This is the ground rent paid by leaseholders. The rate is set locally by each pod through a 'Liquid Governance' (delegated voting) model, allowing for sovereign local economic policy.

Section 4: The Engine of Collective Action: The 'Stigmergic Atlas'

All collective projects, mutual aid, and initiatives are funded through Universal Staking on the 'Commonwealth Atlas' web platform.

Mechanism: Any member can propose an initiative by creating a 'Staking Pool' with a CWC funding target. Other members voluntarily stake their CWC to the pools they support. When a pool is funded, the project is executed.

Function: This allows for the emergent, non-bureaucratic funding of all social goods, including 'Land Stewardship Bounties', 'Community Safety Nets', 'Commons Fellowships', and legal defense. "Voting" is done directly with CWC, reflecting the true, expressed will of the community.

Section 5: The Land Trust & Member Rights

Acquisition: The Trust grows via the 'Stigmergic Acquisition' protocol. The DAO can only acquire land when members have voluntarily staked 100% of the capital needed for the purchase in a 'Commonwealth Bond' pool.

Leasehold Allocation: All new residential leaseholds on commons land are allocated via the 'Community Pledged Capital' protocol.

An open auction is held for the leasehold.

Members can stake CWC directly to the bid of a member they wish to support.

This creates a dynamic, transparent contest between individual capital and collective will.

If a supported bidder loses, the capital pledged to them remains available for them to use on a subsequent bid for a fixed period, creating a "community-funded housing voucher."

Liquidity & Mobility: The 'Commons Buy-Back' Protocol ensures a member can always sell their leasehold improvement back to the Trust at a fair appraised value, paid in CWC. This is funded by a new 'Commonwealth Bond' for internal liquidity. Members moving to a non-CWC area are granted a 'Relocation Liquidity Hedge' (zero-fee CWC-to-USD conversion).

Section 6: The Bridge to the Legacy World

Onboarding Capital: The 'Commonwealth Transformation Fund', operating in 'Sealed Cohorts', allows impact investors to contribute fiat. The fund uses this fiat for land acquisition and bootstrap costs. For every dollar spent, investors receive a corresponding stake in CWC, subject to a long-term 'Vested Contribution' schedule. This is a direct conversion of capital, not a loan.

Onboarding Businesses: The 'Partnership Loan' protocol provides new businesses with risk-free, zero-interest USD loans that are repayable in CWC, turning them into active nodes in the local economy.

Onboarding Members: The 'Commonwealth Concierge' program provides a human-centric, "zero-load" onboarding experience, supported by protocols like the 'Bootstrap Dividend' to demonstrate value before requiring engagement.

Section 7: Constitutional Resilience

The system is protected by a 'Living Constitution' containing protocols designed to ensure its long-term survival and adherence to its principles, including the 'Meta-Rule Lock', the 'Analogue Redundancy' (Stone Book), and the 'Chrysalis Protocol' for a graceful dissolution upon completion of its mission.
