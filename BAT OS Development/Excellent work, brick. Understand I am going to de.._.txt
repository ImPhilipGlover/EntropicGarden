Part VII: Economic Architecture

7.1 The Mutual Credit Network (MCN) The core transactional layer is an interest-free, mutual credit system using the Community Work Credit (CWC) as its unit of account. The total CWC balance of the network is always zero. As championed by mutualists like Benjamin Tucker and Kevin Carson, it functions as a "free banking" system where credit is created at cost (zero) by the users themselves. When a farmer gives a carpenter a bushel of apples for fixing his roof, the carpenter gets +10 CWC, and the farmer gets -10 CWC. No money changes hands. No interest is charged. It is a system built on the revolutionary idea that people can, and generally do, trust each other to return a favor. This stands in stark contrast to systems built on coinage, which historically appears to have been invented primarily to pay soldiers to go and hit people from other societies over the head. The MCN, therefore, is a system for facilitating cooperation, not for funding armies. It is, in its own quiet way, an act of profound optimism. It is a good system.

7.2 The Unified Demurrage System: The Heart of the Economy This system is the framework's key innovation, resolving the conflict between the mutualist/Georgist desire to capture land value and the Proudhonian critique of rent. It applies a single, universal principle derived from Silvio Gesell: All idle capital incurs a carrying cost.

CWC Demurrage (Liquid Capital Cost): A small fee (e.g., 0.5% per month) is automatically applied to all positive CWC balances. This is the direct carrying cost of holding liquid capital out of circulation. Mechanism: A backend cron job runs periodically (e.g., daily) to calculate and deduct the demurrage from all user wallets with a positive balance. The logic ensures atomicity, meaning the deduction is a single, indivisible transaction. The collected CWC is transferred to the Community Fund's public wallet address.

Land Use Contribution (LUC) (Fixed Capital Cost): A small, demurrage-like fee is applied to the registered Use-Lease itself, assessed against the land's value. This is the carrying cost of holding fixed capital (land) for one's exclusive use. It is functionally a holding fee, not rent, as it is paid to the entire community and immediately returned. Mechanism: The value of each parcel is determined by a transparent, community-governed algorithm on SNOW that considers factors like size, location, and access to services. The LUC is calculated as a percentage of this value and is paid in CWC. This payment is also automatically deducted from the leaseholder's CWC wallet and transferred to the Community Fund.

The Community Fund & Citizen's Dividend: All CWC collected from both CWC Demurrage and the LUC flows into a single, transparent, publicly auditable Community Fund wallet. The entire balance of this fund is then automatically and equally redistributed to all verified MCN members on a regular basis (e.g., weekly) as a Citizen's Dividend. Mechanism: This creates a closed, self-funding, and non-extractive loop. The carrying cost of capital doesn't enrich a landlord or a state; it flows directly back to every single member of the community, providing a basic economic floor and constantly stimulating the local economy.

7.3 Credit Generation: Fully Decentralized via P2P Project Funding The centralized HICL/ECL model is abolished. All large-scale credit is now created through a fully peer-to-peer platform, embodying the mutual banking ideals of William Greene and Pierre-Joseph Proudhon. Mechanism: A member posts a detailed proposal for a large-scale project (a new business, community infrastructure) to the "Project Incubation Hub" on SNOW. This includes a business plan, a CWC budget, and a repayment plan. Other MCN members review the proposal. They can ask questions, suggest amendments, and finally, pledge CWC to fund it. This is not a donation; it is a loan offer. When pledges meet the project's funding goal, the credit is created. The project's CWC account is debited the full amount (e.g., -10,000 CWC), and the accounts of all the lenders are credited their respective pledged amounts (summing to +10,000 CWC). The project then uses this CWC to fulfill its own mapped needs (paying laborers, buying materials). As the project generates revenue, it repays its negative balance over time.

7.4 Land & Tenure: The Dynamic Use Model The Community Land Cooperative (CLC) registers Use-Leases based on the principle of possession through use. Use-Lease Auctions: The system for determining the LUC is a market mechanism. Any member can bid what they believe the annual land rent of a parcel is worth. The current leaseholder must either match the bid (and begin paying that LUC to the community fund) or cede the lease to the bidder in exchange for direct payment for 100% of the market value of their improvements on the land. This ensures land migrates to its most highly-valued use and the community captures the full rental value.

7.5 Capital Formation Reputation-Staked Credit: An individual's credit limit is not automatic. It is the sum total of CWC that other members have personally and knowingly "staked" against their reputation. If the debtor defaults, the stakers cover the loss, making credit a direct, P2P, risk-assessed relationship. Decentralized Autonomous Ventures (DAVs): For-profit, equity-based enterprises. Founders can raise capital by issuing and selling "Venture Shares" to members, who then share in the profits and governance of the venture. Federated Projects: A "DAV of DAVs". A structure for multiple DAVs to pool resources for massive, temporary industrial projects while retaining their individual sovereignty. Commons Guilds: A formal, non-profit organizational structure for the social economy. Guilds (e.g., tool libraries, childcare co-ops) are member-governed and can be funded by the Public Goods Fund to manage common-pool resources and mutual aid.
