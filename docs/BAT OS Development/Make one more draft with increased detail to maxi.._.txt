Creating the C.O.M.M.O.N.W.E.A.L.T.H.: A Deep Dive into Regenerative Local Economies and Model Mechanics

Introduction: The Vision of a Regenerative Future

The Bend Regenerative Commonwealth (BRC) is a pragmatic, integrated socio-economic model designed to address systemic challenges like housing unaffordability, economic precarity, and ecological degradation. It aims to empower local communities to reclaim control over essential aspects of their economy – land and money – fostering resilience, equity, and sustainable stewardship. The BRC is a phased, pragmatic, and replicable system that builds trust and utility incrementally, serving as the foundational "Lighthouse Node" for a broader

Confederation of Common Wealths (C.O.M.M.O.N. W.E.A.L.T.H.). 1

I. Fundamental Principles: The Guiding Philosophy

The BRC operates on principles designed for broad appeal, balancing individual liberty with collective responsibility: 2

Local Self-Determination: Empowering communities to make decisions locally, fostering ownership and reducing reliance on external authorities. 3


Economic Freedom & Resilience: Providing access to debt-free capital and affordable housing, liberating individuals and businesses from predatory debt and speculative markets. 4This resonates deeply with

Proudhon's mutualist vision of economic liberty, where individuals are free from the burdens of interest and rent, and Josiah Warren's concept of Equitable Commerce, based on cost as the limit of price and voluntary exchange5.


Fiscal Responsibility & Prudence: Operating with transparency, zero-fee transactions, and sustainable funding mechanisms for long-term stability. 6


Shared Prosperity & Inclusivity: Ensuring benefits are broadly distributed, creating opportunities for all members. 7


Stewardship of the Commons: Recognizing land and natural resources as a shared inheritance, managed responsibly and regeneratively. 8This aligns with the principles of

Georgism in acknowledging the social value of land, though implemented via a CLT's direct acquisition rather than a universal land value tax9.


Voluntary Participation: The system's growth relies on informed, willing engagement, driven by demonstrable benefits. 10This is a core tenet of

individualist anarchism, particularly as articulated by Benjamin Tucker, emphasizing that true order arises from voluntary association rather than coercive authority11.

The BRC's strength lies in the synergistic interaction of its primary components: 12

A. The Community Land Trust (CLT): The Foundational Asset Steward

Function: A non-profit entity that acquires and holds land in perpetuity, separating land ownership from building ownership. It leases land via

Ground Leases to homeowners, ensuring permanent affordability through a Resale Formula for the building. 13This core mechanism is directly inspired by the

Community Land Trust movement pioneered by Robert Swann, which seeks to decommodify land and ensure its long-term stewardship for community benefit14.

Strategic Link to CWC (Commonwealth Credit):

Home Improvement Credit Lines (HICLs): These are the primary CWC injection mechanism into the local economy15151515. CLT administrators, utilizing the SN❄W platform, issue substantial CWC-denominated HICLs to approved homeowners16161616. This creates immediate demand for local labor and materials within the MCN, ensuring that newly created CWC directly translates into productive economic activity tied to tangible assets17171717.


Model Mechanics: The backend logic on SN❄W includes API endpoints (/api/clt/hicl_issue POST) for HICL issuance, tracking of HICL balances, and basic repayment schedules. The database schema (
hicl_id, homeowner_id, amount, terms, status, issued_at) ensures detailed tracking and auditable issuance18. This module acts as the initial "pump" of CWC into the system, aligning with the concept of

social credit being created for specific, beneficial purposes.

Ground Rent (CWC Demand Anchor): Leaseholders pay ground rent in CWC, creating consistent, non-discretionary demand for CWC, anchoring its value to shelter19191919. This mechanism directly incorporates a core idea from

Georgism – that the value of land (or the economic rent derived from it) should accrue to the community rather than private landlords20. By denominating ground rent in CWC, it creates a fundamental, non-speculative utility for the currency.


Model Mechanics: SN❄W will include a backend logic for ground rent calculation and payment processing, and a frontend UI for leaseholders to view and pay their obligations21212121. An automated payment scheduling and notification system ensures timely collection22. This creates a continuous

demand sink for CWC, ensuring its intrinsic value is tied to a fundamental human need.

B. The Mutual Credit Network (MCN) & Commonwealth Credit (CWC): The Circulatory System

community-governed, adaptive peg that directly addresses the concern about how the CWBE adapts if the "supply of goods in the CWBE fluctuates"74. This formalizes how the "average cost" for the CWBE is determined and updated75.

The current understanding suggests a soft peg, which acknowledges the organic nature of local markets while providing a clear reference point79. Transparency & Reporting: Enhance SN❄W's public dashboards to show CEPG transaction volume and stewardship contributions in real-time, offering "stigmergic cues" about the health of the fiat interface89. This provides real-time data for the community to monitor the bridge's activity.
